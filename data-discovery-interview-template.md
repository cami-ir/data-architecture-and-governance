# Data Discovery Interview Template (Philanthropy Team)

## Purpose

These conversations are designed to understand:

- how data is actually used in daily work  
- where data is trusted vs. not trusted  
- where manual work and inefficiencies exist  
- how data supports Philanthropy strategy and goals  

This information directly informs:
- validation rules  
- data flows  
- governance decisions  

---

## Interview Structure

### 1. Role & Systems Context

- What is your role on the Philanthropy team?
- What systems do you use regularly?
  - (e.g., DonorPerfect, exports, spreadsheets, etc.)

---

### 2. How You Use Data

- What are the main things you use data for?
  - reporting
  - outreach
  - segmentation
  - events
  - other

- What data fields do you rely on most?
  - (be as specific as possible)

---

### 3. Address-Specific Questions (Pilot Focus)

- When you look at an address, what makes you trust it?
- Which system do you rely on most for addresses?
- When do you notice addresses are incorrect?
- What do you do when you find a bad address?
- Are there cases where you override one system with another?

---

### 4. Workflow Walkthrough

- Walk me through a recent task where you used this data

- Where in that process:
  - did you have to clean or fix data?
  - did something not match expectations?
  - did you need to double-check another system?

---

### 5. Manual Work & Workarounds

- Do you export data and modify it manually?
- Do you re-enter data between systems?
- Are there steps you repeat regularly that feel inefficient?

---

### 6. Data Quality & Trust

- What data do you trust the most?
- What data do you not trust?
- Are there fields that are used inconsistently?
- Are there known data issues the team works around?

---

### 7. Decision-Making

- When data conflicts between systems:
  - how do you decide what is correct?

- Are there rules of thumb you use?

---

### 8. Gaps & Needs

- What data do you wish you had but don’t?
- What data exists but isn’t usable?
- What would make your work noticeably easier?

---

### 9. Closing Question

- If we could fix one thing about your data, what would it be?

## Key Principle

Focus on **what people actually do**, not what systems are supposed to do.

This discovery phase ensures that:
- validation rules reflect real-world usage  
- data flows align with actual workflows  
- governance is grounded in practice, not theory
